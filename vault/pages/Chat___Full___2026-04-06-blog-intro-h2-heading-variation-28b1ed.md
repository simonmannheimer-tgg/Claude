## My recommendation: Don't rotate the H2 heading.

### Why

**1. You don't gain measurable SEO value from variation**

AI Overviews, featured snippets, and passage indexing don't reward heading diversity for its own sake. Google reads the *structure* (is there a summary? are the bullets semantically rich?) and the *content quality*. Swapping "Key Takeaways" for "Fast Facts You Need to Know" doesn't change either.

**2. Rotation creates consistency debt**

You now have 14 heading variants to QA across hundreds of articles. That's 14 ways for a future writer to miss the rule, 14 strings to audit in Screaming Frog, and 14 opportunities for someone to ask "why does this one say 'Important Points' and that one say 'Quick Summary'?" when troubleshooting.

**3. Users scan for predictable structure**

A consistent H2 trains repeat visitors. They know where the summary lives. The moment you rotate headings, you're optimising for a search engine theory while degrading the user shortcut you've already built.

**4. "No one knows what AI wants anyway" cuts both ways**

If the rules are unclear, the rational move is to pick the clearest, most defensible structure and hold it. Rotating headings because "maybe AI prefers variety" is speculative. Keeping one heading because "users and editors need consistency" is concrete.

---

### What you should do instead

**Ship it with one heading.** Pick the cleanest: **"Key Takeaways From This Article"**. It's neutral, descriptive, and matches the content block's function.

If you're worried about staleness, rotate the *bullet content and phrasing* instead. Vary sentence structure, flip passive to active, lead with different data points. That's where freshness actually lives.

---

### The principle

Consistency isn't the enemy of AI visibility. Vagueness is. A predictable structure with strong, varied content beats 14 heading variants that all say the same thing in slightly different words.

You already said you're not fussy. Don't create work for a problem you don't have evidence for.
