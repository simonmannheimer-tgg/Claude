**H1 format**

"2026 TVs" for the base page and "2026 [Brand] TVs" for brand pages (e.g. "2026 LG TVs", "2026 Samsung TVs"). Year-leading is stronger for a few reasons.

First, the search intent is year-forward. People search "2026 TVs", "2026 LG TVs", "best TVs 2026". Putting the year first aligns the H1 with the dominant query pattern, which matters for both traditional rankings and AI answer extraction. "[Brand] TVs 2026" reads more like a sentence tail than a search phrase.

Second, it mirrors how the industry frames new model years. CES coverage, review sites, and comparison articles all lead with the year ("2026 Samsung QLED lineup", "2026 LG OLED range"). Matching that framing helps with topical alignment.

Third, it future-proofs the annual rollover. Every year you just swap the number. "Latest" is ambiguous mid-year when 2025 and 2026 models coexist on shelves, whereas "2026" is unambiguous.
