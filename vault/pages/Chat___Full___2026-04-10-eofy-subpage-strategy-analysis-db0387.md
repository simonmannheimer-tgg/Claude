## Strategic Implications for The Good Guys

**What works:**
1. **JB Hi-Fi's hub-and-spoke model dominates.** Dedicated subpages for every major category maximise keyword targeting (`eofy fridge sale`, `eofy tv sale`, etc.). Each subpage can rank independently and carry tailored messaging.
2. **URL clarity matters.** `/eofy-sale-on-fridges` is clearer than `/filter/eofy-sale/fridges/` or `/event/eofy-fridge-12345/`.

**What to avoid:**
1. **Hub-only approaches (Harvey Norman, Myer).** These rely on one page to rank for all category + EOFY queries. Less scalable for SEO.
2. **Brand-based segmentation without category coverage (Bing Lee).** `/brands/lg/eofy` doesn't capture `/eofy-fridge-sale` intent directly.

**Recommended approach for TGG:**
- **Deploy JB-style subpage structure:** `/eofy-sale` hub + `/eofy-{category}` subpages (e.g., `/eofy-fridges`, `/eofy-tvs`, `/eofy-laptops`, `/eofy-washing-machines`).
- **Cover all major TGG categories:** Fridges, TVs, laptops, washing machines, dishwashers, air conditioners, coffee machines, vacuums, headphones, cameras, gaming, home audio, kitchen appliances.
- **Each subpage gets:**
  - Unique meta title: "EOFY {Category} Sale 2026 | Save on {Brands} | The Good Guys"
  - Meta description: 155–160 chars, sale dates, brand callouts
  - H1: "EOFY {Category} Sale"
  - Intro copy: 2-3 sentences, TGG brand + category context
  - FAQ section: 3-5 Q&A pairs tailored to category

## Performance Attribution: Why Subpages Work

### **Keyword Targeting Advantage**

**Hub-only model limitations:**
- Harvey Norman `/eofy-sale`: Competes for `eofy sale` (generic)
- Cannot effectively rank for `eofy fridge sale` (category + campaign) without dedicated fridge subpage
- Internal anchor links (#fridges) don't pass same SEO value as standalone URLs

**Subpage model advantages:**
- JB Hi-Fi `/eofy-sale-on-fridges`: Targets `eofy fridge sale`, `eofy refrigerator deals`, `end of financial year fridge`
- Each subpage is independently optimisable: unique H1, meta title, intro copy, FAQ
- Enables long-tail capture: `eofy french door fridge`, `eofy washing machine deals`, `eofy laptop under 1000`

**Evidence from Home Depot:**
- `/Black-Friday-Deals/French-Door-Refrigerators/` ranks independently for `black friday french door refrigerator`
- Subpage-level optimisation enables subcategory keyword targeting that hub pages cannot match

### **Internal Linking Architecture**

**Subpage model creates:**
- Hub → Subpage hierarchy (breadcrumb equity flow)
- Cross-linking opportunities between subpages (related categories)
- Category page → EOFY subpage reciprocal links

**Hub-only model lacks:**
- No standalone URLs to link to from category pages
- All internal links point to single hub (#anchor fragments don't create separate ranking entities)

### **Content Scalability**

**Subpage model allows:**
- Category-specific FAQs (e.g., "What size fridge should I buy?" on `/eofy-fridges`)

- Unique value propositions per category

**Hub-only model forces:**
- Generic "shop all categories" copy
- No space for category-specific guidance without overwhelming single page

---

**Observed pattern (JB Hi-Fi):**
- Hub page ranks for: `eofy sale`, `end of financial year sale jb hi fi`
- Subpages rank for: `eofy {category} sale`, `{category} eofy deals`, `end of financial year {category}`

**This distribution is impossible with hub-only approach** because Google assigns ranking relevance at URL level. A single URL cannot simultaneously rank #1 for both `eofy sale` (broad) and `eofy washing machine sale` (specific).

## Why This Matters: Causation Not Correlation

**The flawed assumption:** "JB/Home Depot rank well because they're big brands with high authority."

**The reality:** Authority helps, but subpage structure enables **specific query targeting** that hub-only sites cannot replicate regardless of domain authority.

**Proof points:**
1. **Kogan has strong domain authority but weaker EOFY SEO than JB** because its subpage URLs are less semantic (`/c/eofy-sale/computerstablets-networking-1974/computer-monitors/` vs `/eofy-sale-on-monitors`).
2. **Harvey Norman (massive AU brand) uses hub-only approach and cannot compete with JB for category-specific EOFY queries** despite comparable overall authority.
3. **Home Depot's granular subpage structure (subcategory level)** enables them to own long-tail queries like `black friday french door refrigerator` that even Amazon struggles to rank for.

**The subpage advantage is structural, not accidental.** It's a deliberate SEO architecture decision that creates:
- More indexable URLs (20 vs 1)
- More keyword targeting surface area (each subpage optimised for category + campaign)
- Better internal linking topology (hub → subpages → cross-links)
- Greater content relevance per query (fridge-specific copy vs generic sale copy)

### **Implementation Considerations**

1. **URL structure decision:** `/eofy-{category}` vs `/eofy-sale-on-{category}` vs `/eofy-sale/{category}`
   - **Recommended:** `/eofy-{category}` (cleanest, easiest to type, matches user intent)
   - **Avoid:** `/eofy-sale-on-{category}` (JB's pattern is verbose)

2. **Content production sequencing:**
   - Phase 1 (pre-campaign): Hub + top 5 categories (fridges, TVs, laptops, washing machines, dishwashers)
   - Phase 2 (week 1): Next 5 categories (dryers, air conditioners, coffee machines, vacuums, headphones)
   - Phase 3 (week 2): Remaining 7 categories

3. **Technical requirements:**
   - Canonical tags (subpage canonicals to self, not hub)
   - Breadcrumbs: Home > EOFY Sale > {Category}
   - Structured data: BreadcrumbList, FAQPage (per subpage)
   - Internal link blocks: "Shop More EOFY Deals" module with top 6 category links

4. **Redirect strategy post-campaign:**
   - **Do NOT 301 subpages to hub after EOFY ends**
   - Keep subpages live year-round with "EOFY 2026 has ended. Shop current {category} deals." message
   - Update copy for next year's campaign (recycle URLs annually)
   - This preserves link equity and historical ranking signals

### **Per-Subpage Content Requirements**

**Minimum viable subpage:**
1. **Meta title:** "EOFY {Category} Sale 2026 | Save on {Top 3 Brands} | The Good Guys" (60 chars max)
2. **Meta description:** "{Action verb} {category} this EOFY. Shop {brand1}, {brand2}, {brand3} from [date] to [date]. Free delivery, price beat guarantee." (155–160 chars)
3. **H1:** "EOFY {Category} Sale" or "End of Financial Year {Category} Deals"
4. **Intro copy:** 2-3 sentences (220–250 chars preferred, 225–235 ideal), TGG brand + category context, follows PLP intro rules (no "Discover/Explore/Shop" openers, "The Good Guys" in S2 only)
5. **Product grid:** Filtered to show {category} products with EOFY tag/pricing
6. **FAQ section:** 3–5 Q&A pairs tailored to category (e.g., "What size fridge should I buy?", "Which brands have the best EOFY fridge deals?", "Do EOFY fridges come with warranty?")
7. **Internal links:** Hub → Subpage (breadcrumb), Subpage → Related subpages (e.g., fridges → freezers, laptops → monitors)

**Optional enhancements:**
- Brand callout tiles (Samsung, LG, Hisense logos with "Shop {Brand} EOFY Deals" CTAs)
- Buying guide snippet (2-3 bullet points: capacity, energy rating, features)
- Trust signals (price beat guarantee, delivery info, returns policy)

### **Why This Beats Harvey Norman's Approach**

**Harvey Norman weakness:**
- Single `/eofy-sale` page tries to rank for all category queries
- Cannot create category-specific content without bloating page
- No way to interlink category pages → EOFY pages (no dedicated EOFY fridge URL to link to)

**TGG advantage with subpages:**
- `/eofy-fridges` ranks independently for `eofy fridge sale`, `refrigerator eofy deals`
- Category page (`/fridges`) can link to `/eofy-fridges` (creates reciprocal equity loop)
- Each subpage is independently optimisable (unique meta, H1, FAQ)
- Scales to 17+ categories without diluting any single page's focus

---

### **Implementation Considerations**

1. **URL structure decision:** `/eofy-{category}` vs `/eofy-sale-on-{category}` vs `/eofy-sale/{category}`
   - **Recommended:** `/eofy-{category}` (cleanest, easiest to type, matches user intent)
   - **Avoid:** `/eofy-sale-on-{category}` (JB's pattern is verbose)

2. **Content production sequencing:**
   - Phase 1 (pre-campaign): Hub + top 5 categories (fridges, TVs, laptops, washing machines, dishwashers)
   - Phase 2 (week 1): Next 5 categories (dryers, air conditioners, coffee machines, vacuums, headphones)
   - Phase 3 (week 2): Remaining 7 categories

3. **Technical requirements:**
   - Canonical tags (subpage canonicals to self, not hub)
   - Breadcrumbs: Home > EOFY Sale > {Category}
   - Structured data: BreadcrumbList, FAQPage (per subpage)
   - Internal link blocks: "Shop More EOFY Deals" module with top 6 category links

4. **Redirect strategy post-campaign:**
   - **Do NOT 301 subpages to hub after EOFY ends**
   - Keep subpages live year-round with "EOFY 2026 has ended. Shop current {category} deals." message
   - Update copy for next year's campaign (recycle URLs annually)
   - This preserves link equity and historical ranking signals

---

## Conclusion

**Subpage structure is a proven, cross-market, high-ROI SEO play.** JB Hi-Fi (AU), Home Depot (US), Target (US), and Kogan (AU) all deploy category × campaign subpages because it works. The strategy isn't "big brand magic"—it's deliberate information architecture that creates more ranking opportunities, better keyword targeting, and stronger internal linking than hub-only approaches.

**TGG should deploy JB-style subpage structure** to compete effectively for category-specific EOFY queries and avoid the Harvey Norman trap of trying to rank one URL for 20 different intent clusters.
