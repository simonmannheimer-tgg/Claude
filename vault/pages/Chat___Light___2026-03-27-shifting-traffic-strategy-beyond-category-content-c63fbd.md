## Summary

**Conversation overview**

Simon Mannheimer is a TGG (The Good Guys) SEO Strategist working with the Overdose agency to build the February 2026 Monthly SEO Update presentation deck. The session focused on narrative planning, data analysis, and chart creation for the deck rather than PPTX building, following a previous session where slide building attempts produced off-brand results. Simon was explicit and direct throughout — correcting Claude multiple times on approach, tone, and output quality, including frustration when charts were poor quality, when the wrong chart type was used (bar instead of line), and when Claude misread which slide was being referenced in the audit.

The core narrative agreed upon for the deck is a three-act story: TGG recovered from a site migration (proven by January's -0.71% YoY non-brand clicks after being at -25% mid-2025), then recovered into a changed landscape where CTR at every ranking position has been compressed by new SERP features (AI Overviews, shopping carousels, Reddit), and is now winning in the new channels (AI Visibility #1 among competitors, Owned AIO Keywords nearly double nearest competitor, Organic Shopping revenue stable at $27-28K/day). Key proof points discussed include the AccuRanker SoV chart showing stable rankings, the GSC screenshots showing impressions recovering faster than clicks (confirming CTR compression not ranking loss), the slow cooker SERP screenshot showing TGG at organic rank 3 but visible rank 16, the enterprise client grid showing the same pattern across six other Overdose clients, and the james_od Slack message as external validation.

Data sources analysed included GA4 CSV data (daily organic search and organic shopping sessions and revenue), the AIO/product shopping market share xlsx (AI metrics, GMC data, GSC trends, competitor Owned AIO keywords), the CTR analysis xlsx (position 1-11 CTR by month March 2025 to February 2026), and the MD report PDF. Key findings extracted: position 1 CTR dropped from 38.07% to 17.5% YoY (-54%), organic revenue was +5.6% YoY despite sessions down 10%, GMC purchases grew +80% MoM January to February, TGG AI Visibility at 18.9% vs HN 14.6% vs AO 12.2% vs JB 10.3%, and Owned AIO Keywords at 9,300 vs competitors clustered around 5,000. Simon corrected Claude's use of the SEMRush Organic Shopping keyword footprint metric, noting it shows keywords that have a shopping result not whether TGG appears in it, and preferred GMC clicks and purchases instead. For the organic shopping daily revenue comparison, Simon confirmed the honest framing — stability not growth — was the right story after Claude flagged the data showed flat to slightly declining daily averages Jan vs Feb.

Charts were built as interactive HTML artifacts using Chart.js with Poppins font, white background, and TGG brand colours: #1553A2 (primary blue), #ED1C24 (red), #FF9900 (orange/highlight), with competitor brand colours Harvey Norman #E8261B, Appliances Online #00A6CE, JB Hi-Fi #FFB800. Simon's consistent preference was for clean, simple charts — single metric where possible, no dual axis unless essential, value labels only on key data points, figsize equivalent of (12,5). Saved artifacts include owned_aio_competitor_comparison.html, ai_visibility_competitor.html (incomplete), and gmc_daily_sessions_revenue.html. A final deck audit was conducted on a newer version of the PPTX, with Claude incorrectly flagging a contradiction between slides 2 and 6 — Simon corrected that slide 2 covers February and slide 6 covers March recovery, which is sequential not contradictory. The session ended with Simon asking for forward-looking strategy bullet points and asking Claude to recall the agreed rename for the slide titled "Monthly SEO Overview - Changes in Search, non-brand clicks hit especially hard," which Claude could not locate in the conversation history.
