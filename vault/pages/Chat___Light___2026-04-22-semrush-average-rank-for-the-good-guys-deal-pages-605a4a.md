## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

The person is conducting SEO analysis work focused on The Good Guys Australia (thegoodguys.com.au), a retail electronics and appliance brand. They were working with a Semrush export file called `semrush_june2025.xlsx` and asked Claude to extract average keyword rankings for a specific set of 25 sale and deals pages, then to analyze sales keywords grouped by product category regardless of URL, and finally to output the results directly into the Excel file with proof-level detail.

The initial request revealed that 23 of the 25 target deals subcategory URLs (e.g., `/deals/air-conditioners-and-fans`, `/deals/tvs`) had no data in the file, with only `/deals` and `/eofy-sale` appearing across the four sheets. Claude surfaced this gap and flagged staging environment URLs (shopifyuat domain) appearing as noise. For the keyword-by-category analysis, Claude built a regex-based categorisation mapping across 20 product categories aligned to The Good Guys' deals taxonomy, then aggregated keyword count, average position, total search volume, and total traffic per category. Key findings included Fridges, TVs, and Laptops driving the highest traffic, Dishwashers punching above their keyword count, and Gaming/Audio sitting at weak average positions around 10.

When the person requested the results "in the sheet with proof," Claude added two new sheets to the original Excel file without modifying existing sheets: a styled "Category Summary" tab with the rolled-up metrics sorted by total traffic, and a "Sales Keywords Categorised" tab containing all 1,431 individual keywords with their assigned category, position, volume, traffic, and URL, colour-coded by category for visual auditing. The file was saved as `semrush_june2025_categorised.xlsx`.
