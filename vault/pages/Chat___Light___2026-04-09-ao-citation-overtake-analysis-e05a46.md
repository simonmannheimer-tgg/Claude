## Summary

**Conversation overview**

The user is an SEO/digital marketing professional working on a monthly MD (Managing Director) report for The Good Guys (TGG), an Australian consumer electronics and appliances retailer. The conversation focused on analysing AI citation performance data from the Profound platform to explain why competitor Appliances Online (AO) overtook TGG in total AI citation market share (3.2% vs 3.0%) for March 2026, and how to report this honestly alongside traditional SEO metrics. Other competitors tracked include Harvey Norman (HN), JB Hi-Fi (JB), and Bing Lee.

The user worked through multiple iterations of report copy, consistently pushing back on narrative-driven or inaccurate analysis. Key corrections included: catching fabricated query growth figures (+117% was a partial vs full month comparison error, real growth was +5%/day); rejecting the claim TGG had zero blog content (TGG uses /whats-new/ and /buying-guide/ URL formats); insisting all platform citation comparisons show both TGG and AO figures, not just one competitor; removing revenue references after stating this once; and flagging when claimed data points (like "despite TGG's larger editorial inventory") contradicted what was just established. The user also repeatedly corrected length, punctuation style (AU grammar, no em-dashes), and tone, preferring concise factual reporting over interpretive narrative.

The final agreed copy covers: traditional metric recovery (+10.3% MoM sessions, -25.6% YoY non-branded clicks); AI recapture projects working (AIO +22.6%, LLM sessions +2.4%, 5.1pp visibility lead); AO taking the #1 citation rank (3.2% vs 3.0%) while JB (-0.5pp) and HN (-0.3pp) declined; AO's advantage linked to "best/top selling/popular [category]" content format efficiency (AO ~340 URLs at 7.7 citations/URL vs TGG ~70 URLs at 5.3 citations/URL); platform-specific evidence on ChatGPT (AO 2,050 vs TGG 1,206); and a next steps recommendation to scope a "Top Selling/Popular [Product Category]" blog strategy across high-citation categories. The user's preferred abbreviations are JB and HN, not full brand names.
