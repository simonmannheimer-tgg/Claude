## Summary

**Conversation overview**

The person works in SEO and was preparing to present a Black Friday/Cyber Monday (BFCM) 2025 performance review for The Good Guys, produced in partnership with a colleague named Simon M and Overdose Digital. They uploaded a PowerPoint presentation and asked Claude to create "queue cards" (speaker notes) for each of the 21 slides. The deck covered organic search performance during BFCM 2025, root cause analysis of a significant session and non-brand click decline, competitive benchmarking against JB Hi-Fi and others, and three strategic recommendations for winning BFCM 2026.

Claude initially produced detailed bullet-point queue cards in a formal, prose-heavy style. The person then asked for the cards to be rewritten to match their tone of voice — shorter, more direct, data-first, with less hedging — and to be easier to scan. Claude rewrote all 21 slides accordingly. The person then asked for colour coding and visual separation, plus a spoken paragraph per slide alongside flexible bullet points for improvisation. Claude produced a final HTML file with colour-coded slide numbers and section tags (blue for opening, grey for context, red for impact, green for proof, purple for benchmark, amber for recommendations, slate for appendix), a white spoken paragraph block with a colour-matched left border, and bullet points beneath for ad-lib use. The person's communication style is concise and direct, preferring scannable formats over dense prose, and they wanted a document usable both on screen and in print.
