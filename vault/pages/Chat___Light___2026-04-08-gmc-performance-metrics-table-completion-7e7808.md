## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

The person is working on a digital marketing performance reporting project, building monthly performance tables for GMC (Google Merchant Center) and organic shopping channels. The conversation focused on two distinct tasks: first, cross-checking the accuracy of an existing GMC performance table spanning Jul-25 through Feb-26 and filling in March 2026 data; second, building a new organic shopping performance table using GA4 export data.

For the GMC table, Claude processed an uploaded CSV to extract March 2026 actuals and verified all existing monthly figures. Several errors were identified in the person's original table: CTR month-over-month calculations were incorrect across multiple months, the Feb-26 Purchase Rate MoM figure was slightly off, and the Jan-26 CTR MoM had a minor rounding discrepancy. The completed GMC table covers Clicks, Impressions, CTR, Purchases, and Purchase Rate with MoM changes for all months through Mar-26.

For the organic shopping table, the person clarified that tracking was only fixed from approximately January 17, 2026, making earlier data unreliable. Claude filtered the GA4 data to Jan 17 onward, extrapolated the 15 days of clean January data to a full 31-day month using daily averages, and built a three-month table (Jan-26 through Mar-26) covering Sessions, Revenue, and Revenue per Session with MoM changes. April 2026 was excluded as a partial month. The person should be aware that January figures are estimated extrapolations and should be flagged as such in stakeholder presentations. Key metrics from the final organic shopping table show sessions and revenue declining across the three months, while revenue per session improved in February before a slight pullback in March.
