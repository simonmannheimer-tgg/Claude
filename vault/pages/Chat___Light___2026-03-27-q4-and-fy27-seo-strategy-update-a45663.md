The -54% figure was also flagged for seasonality distortion given the comparison window spans BFCM. The final email uses "~50-60% less SEO traffic click-through" as the defensible framing sourced from Overdose's analysis.

Simon has a clear and consistent formatting preference for the email's data lines: lead with the metric label and all key data points before a semicolon, then follow with explanatory context after it, matching the style of lines like "February Non-branded Clicks -34.90% MoM, -22.2% YoY amid ranking fluctuations and CTR Compression; [explanation]." He pushed back multiple times when lines were too long before the semicolon or when data was buried in the explanatory section. He also prefers confident, direct framing for exec audiences and flagged "diminishing ROI from traditional organic listings" as too defensive, accepting "evolving our priorities in response to structural changes in search" instead. He corrected "Alison and I" to "Alison and me" when Claude raised it, and confirmed the closing bullet list was complete after Claude checked the source deck for missing workstreams.
