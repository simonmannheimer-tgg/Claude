__Meeting Summary__
Purpose:  Align on whether to create EOFY category pages and confirm where effort should be focused for EOFY 2026 to maximise traffic and revenue.
 
Key Decision:
We will NOT create dedicated EOFY category pages (e.g. EOFY TV Sale, EOFY Fridge Sale) for EOFY 2026.
Instead, we will:

* Keep EOFY as a hub‑led strategy
* Use the EOFY hub to route traffic to strong existing category sale pages (EOFY hub optimisation)
* Focus SEO effort on improving performance for high‑volume generic “sale” terms
 
Reasoning:
 
1. EOFY category demand is very small relative to generic demand

* EOFY + category searches make up a tiny share of total demand
* Example (TVs):
   * Generic “TV sale / TV sales / TV deals”: ~200k searches
   * EOFY TV terms: ~1–2k searches
* This pattern is consistent across most categories
 
2. The EOFY hub already captures almost all EOFY intent

* Current data shows the EOFY hub receives ~99% of impressions and clicks for EOFY‑intent queries
* Category pages rarely surface for EOFY queries
 
3.The real performance gap is generic “sale” terms

* The most concerning finding:
   * High‑volume generic terms (e.g. “TV sale”, “TV sales”) are much larger than EOFY terms
   * The Good Guys currently ranks below key competitors on these queries
* Improving rankings here delivers value:
   * During EOFY
   * And for the entire year
__This is where effort will return the most value.__
 
 
__What we WILL do for EOFY 2026__
Priority 1 - Strengthen generic category sale performance
Priority 2 - Use EOFY hub as a traffic router - Link prominently to ranked generic sale/category pages (full EOFY hub optimisation is required)
 
Summary:  For EOFY 2026, the opportunity is not to create more EOFY pages, but to make our strongest category sale pages harder to beat. EOFY should funnel demand into those pages. Improving generic “sale” rankings will deliver far more value than building EOFY‑specific category pages.
 
__Post‑EOFY Monitoring Plan__- After EOFY 2026, we will actively monitor the EOFY hub’s performance
If post‑EOFY analysis shows that:

* EOFY category queries reach meaningful scale and
* Competitors are outperforming us specifically due to structural category EOFY pages and
* Hub‑based routing no longer captures incremental demand efficiently
We will reassess creating a limited set of EOFY category pages, starting with (TBA):

* Laptops
* Phones
* TVs
* Fridges
* Washing Machines
( suggestion is based on search volume and sales data)
 
This would be:

* Selective, not blanket
* Reusable year‑on‑year
* Designed to complement (not replace) the EOFY hub
 
Summary:  Post‑event, we will closely monitor EOFY hub performance and competitor behaviour. If evidence shows that category‑specific EOFY pages become necessary to protect or grow traffic share, we have a clear, data‑driven pathway to introduce them without compromising SEO foundations.
