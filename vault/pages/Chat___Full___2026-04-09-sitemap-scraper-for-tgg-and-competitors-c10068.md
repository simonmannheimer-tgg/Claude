## Your current notebook isn't fit for this purpose

**Problems:**

1. **No competitor differentiation** — You're scraping TGG sitemaps only. Adding JB/Harvey Norman/Appliances Online URLs to the `SITEMAPS` list will work mechanically, but you'll have no way to segment, compare, or track what came from where beyond a "Sitemap" column that just says "product_sitemap_3.xml".

2. **Wrong data model for competitive intelligence** — You're collecting:
   - Canonical URL (useless for gap analysis)
   - Previous breadcrumb URL (marginally useful for understanding hierarchy)
   - HTTP status (nice-to-have, not strategic)

   You're **not** collecting:
   - **Page type** (product vs category vs brand vs content)
   - **Category taxonomy** (what categories does JB have that TGG doesn't?)
   - **URL patterns** (how do they structure /brands/ vs /categories/?)
   - **Metadata** (titles, descriptions — the actual competitive content)
   - **Product/category names** (extracted from breadcrumb text, not just URLs)
   - **Schema markup beyond breadcrumbs** (Product, FAQPage, Review schema)

3. **CSV is the wrong output for monthly tracking** — You'll end up with `sitemap-audit_2026-04-09.csv`, `sitemap-audit_2026-05-09.csv`, `sitemap-audit_2026-06-09.csv` and no automated way to diff them. Manual VLOOKUP hell.

4. **Dashboard requirement conflicts with current approach** — You say you want a dashboard, but you're building a one-shot scraper that dumps to CSV. Where's the dashboard going to pull from? A growing pile of CSVs? You need a persistent data layer (SQLite minimum, Airtable if you want it accessible to stakeholders).

5. **Scraping 35 JB sitemaps + 7 Harvey Norman + 9 Appliances Online + 9 TGG = ~15,000–30,000 URLs** — Your current `MAX_WORKERS=10` will take 30–60 minutes. That's fine for a one-off, **brutal** for monthly runs. You need:
   - Incremental scraping (only check URLs that changed `lastmod`)
   - Persistent storage so you're not re-scraping the entire internet every month

```

scraper.py (fetch + parse + enrich)
    ↓

    ↓
analysis.py (competitive gap detection)
    ↓
dashboard.html (Recharts/Chart.js visualization)
```
