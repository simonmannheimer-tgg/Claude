### PART 3. Bidirectional linking gaps and additional opportunities

These are structural issues with the plan's logic, not just copy fixes.

**Gap 1. None of the three target pages link back to their source pages.**
The plan creates inlinks TO the iPhone 17 Pro, Pro Max, and Air pages from 7 source pages. But none of those target pages are updated to link back toward the blog/guide content. This means the equity flows one way. At minimum, the three target PLPs should have a "further reading" or "related guides" section linking to the blog posts covering them (best mobile phones, amazing smartphone things, EOFY buys). This is standard hub-and-spoke and TGG's own mega-menu strategy mirrors this logic.

**Gap 2. The iPhone category PLP (`/iphone`) is never the source page, only a link destination.**
The most powerful linking source for new iPhone models is the parent category page itself. The plan doesn't touch it. Adding a "New this year" or "Latest releases" module to `https://www.thegoodguys.com.au/phones-and-wearables/mobile-phones/iphone` linking to all five current models (17, 17e, 17 Pro, 17 Pro Max, Air) would be the single highest-impact addition to this plan.

**Gap 3. iPhone 17e has no inlinks in this plan at all.**
Across all 7 pages, iPhone 17e is never mentioned. If it's a current model with its own PLP, this is a significant oversight. It should appear alongside the other models in every section that references the "current iPhone range."

**Gap 4. The sitemap CSV wasn't accessible** (upload didn't persist), so I can't confirm what other relevant pages exist. Re-upload it and I'll identify additional sources. Likely candidates based on TGG site structure would be: `/phones-and-wearables`, `/apple`, any `/whats-new/` articles covering Apple or camera phones, and any `/deals/` pages with Apple.
