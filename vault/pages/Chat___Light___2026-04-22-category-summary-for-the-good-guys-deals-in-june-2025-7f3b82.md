## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

The person requested a category-level performance summary for a list of specific TGG (The Good Guys) /deals/ URL categories for June 2025, providing 23 category URLs spanning appliances, technology, kitchen, health, and home categories. Claude worked with uploaded GMC (Google Merchant Center) report data covering May–June 2025, which included Google product category taxonomy (up to five levels deep) but no native TGG URL/category column despite the filename suggesting otherwise.

Claude built a mapping from TGG /deals/ slugs to Google product category hierarchies, then aggregated June 2025 impressions, clicks, and CTR for each mapped TGG category. The final output was a ranked table of 23 categories with impressions ranging from 4.7M (kitchen/kitchen-appliances) down to ~16K (kitchenware-sinks-and-taps), with the bundle category flagged as unmapped. Claude also documented important structural caveats: kitchen and kitchen-appliances mapped to nearly identical Google categories (differing only by the kitchenware-sinks-and-taps subset); fridge-and-laundry fully overlapped with the sum of fridges, washing-machines, and dryers; technology overlapped with laptops, phone-and-smart-watches, and smart-home; and the relatively low air-conditioners-and-fans figure (277K impressions) was attributed to AU winter seasonality driving traffic toward heaters instead.

Key domain terminology used includes GMC report, Google product category levels (1st–4th), CTR, impressions, clicks, and TGG /deals/ slugs. The data files worked with were `GMC_REPORT_MAY-JUNE_WITH_CATEGORY.csv`, `split_half_1.csv`, and `split_half_2.csv`, with the GMC report being the primary source for this analysis.
