## What this copy is doing and why format matters

**Purpose of PLP intro copy** (establishing this clearly first):

The 2-sentence intro is not marketing. It is a semantic anchor. Its job is to:
1. Tell search engines and AI systems what this page is definitively *about* in the clearest, most entity-rich terms possible
2. Confirm navigational intent for users who already know the brand. they're not browsing, they've arrived.
3. Pack enough specific product entities (formats, tech names, capacities, sub-types) into a small character budget that the page can rank across a wide cluster of long-tail queries, not just the head term

---

## What the research tells us about AI citability

ChatGPT is more likely to cite content that uses definite language, not vague language, and has high entity density. The optimal paragraph length for AI extraction is 40–60 words, so each semantic chunk can stand alone as a citable unit and comprehensively answer a potential query.

AI systems don't browse filters. They extract structured meaning. If your category page cannot explain itself without interaction, it becomes invisible inside AI shopping answers. This is why listing product sub-types in the copy itself matters, rather than relying on facet navigation.

Pages ranking for multiple fan-out queries are far more likely to earn AI Overview citations. More specific entities in the copy means more query surface, which increases citation probability directly.

ChatGPT cites retailers roughly 36% of the time, a 9x difference over Google AIOs, because ChatGPT has to serve both the research and purchase need in one response. For TGG specifically, LLM citability matters more than AIO citability at the PLP level, because users asking "where can I buy a Hisense fridge" get directed to retailer pages by ChatGPT, not by Google's AIO.

### Avoid Repetitive Patterns

## Two types of restriction — understand the difference

This file contains two distinct categories of rule. Confusing them causes 
both over-restriction and under-restriction.

**Hard bans** — these words and phrases must never appear, period. They 
exist for SEM compliance, legal, or factual accuracy reasons. No exceptions.

**Overuse warnings** — these words are permitted. The restriction is on 
*frequency and context*: don't repeat them within a single piece, and don't 
let them dominate a batch. The purpose is variety and specificity, not 
blanket avoidance.

## Hard Bans — Never Use

| Banned | Why | Use Instead |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| sale / sales | SEM compliance | deal / deals |
| save / saving **when framing price** (e.g. "save money", "save on", "save with Price Beat") | SEM compliance | pay less / get a great deal on |
| discount | SEM compliance | deal / offer |
| exclusive (re: deals) | Legal risk | best deals, great deals |
| Australia's trusted choice | SEM compliance | pay less at The Good Guys |
| quality brands | SEM compliance | big brands, best brands |
| get great value | SEM compliance | get a great deal on [product] |
| perfect for all needs | Too vague | be specific: perfect for your home / the whole family |
| chat to our staff/team | SEM compliance | chat to our experts in store / our friendly team members |
| amazing, stunning, wonderful | Oversold, generic | name the actual benefit |
| busy homes, hearty meals, sleek design | Cliché, AI-sounding | describe the actual scenario or feature |

**Critical distinction on "save/saving":** The ban applies only when "save" 
frames a price or cost benefit — "save money," "save on [product]," "save 
with Price Beat." Using "save time," "save space," or "save energy" in a 
functional/outcome context is not a violation. When in doubt: is this making 
a price claim? If yes, rewrite. If no, it is fine.

### Energy Claims

  (e.g. heat pump dryers). Do not blanket-apply to non-efficient categories.
- Never combine energy claims with "saving money" — false green messaging.
- "Efficient dryers" = heat pump dryers only. Not all dryers qualify.

- Never use "save with Price Beat" — this is a hard ban (price framing).
- Use "pay less with Price Beat Online" or "get a great deal with Price Beat 
  Online".
- Confirm Price Beat applicability before mentioning.

- Cannot be used on big and bulky products.
- Best for gifting products.

These are permitted words. They become a problem when repeated within a 
single piece or when they dominate a batch. The fix is always variety and 
specificity — not removal.

| Word/Phrase | Restriction | Why |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| premium | Max once per piece | Loses meaning through repetition |
| high-quality | Max once per piece. Often unnecessary | Brands and specs speak for themselves |
| best-in-class | Max once per piece | Feels corporate if overused |
| cutting-edge | Max once per piece | Borderline cliché |
| trusted, reliable | Fine on category PLPs (Type A/D). **Do not use on brand PLPs (Type B/C)** | Makes brand-specific copy feel generic and interchangeable |

## Phrasing Principles

template. This applies at the piece level and across an entire batch.

  format-first, spec-first. Do not open every piece the same way.

- Instead of "great features" → name the actual feature (TwinDos,
