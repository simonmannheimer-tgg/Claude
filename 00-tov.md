# TOV & Language Reference

> **Load this file before every writing task.** It applies to all content types.

---

## Two types of restriction — understand the difference

This file contains two distinct categories of rule. Confusing them causes both over-restriction and under-restriction.

**Hard bans** — these words and phrases must never appear, period. They exist for SEM compliance, legal, or factual accuracy reasons. No exceptions.

**Overuse warnings** — these words are permitted. The restriction is on *frequency and context*: don't repeat them within a single piece, and don't let them dominate a batch. The purpose is variety and specificity, not blanket avoidance.

---

## Hard Bans — Never Use

| Banned | Why | Use Instead |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| sale / sales | SEM compliance | deal / deals |
| save / saving **when framing price** (e.g. "save money", "save on", "save with Price Beat") | SEM compliance | pay less / get a great deal on |
| discount | SEM compliance | deal / offer |
| exclusive (re: deals) | Legal risk | best deals, great deals |
| Australia's trusted choice | SEM compliance | pay less at The Good Guys |
| quality brands | SEM compliance | big brands, best brands |
| get great value | SEM compliance | get a great deal on [product] |
| perfect for all needs | Too vague | be specific: perfect for your home / the whole family |
| chat to our staff/team | SEM compliance | chat to our experts in store / our friendly team members |
| amazing, stunning, wonderful | Oversold, generic | name the actual benefit |
| busy homes, hearty meals, sleek design | Cliché, AI-sounding | describe the actual scenario or feature |

**Critical distinction on "save/saving":** The ban applies only when "save" frames a price or cost benefit — "save money," "save on [product]," "save with Price Beat." Using "save time," "save space," or "save energy" in a functional/outcome context is not a violation. When in doubt: is this making a price claim? If yes, rewrite. If no, it's fine.

### Energy Claims
- "Energy efficient" is OK **only** for genuinely energy-efficient products (e.g. heat pump dryers). Do not blanket-apply to non-efficient categories.
- Never combine energy claims with "saving money" — false green messaging.
- "Efficient dryers" = heat pump dryers only. Not all dryers qualify.

### Price Beat
- Never use "save with Price Beat" → use "pay less with Price Beat Online" or "get a great deal with Price Beat Online".
- Confirm Price Beat applicability before mentioning.

### Click & Collect
- Cannot be used on big and bulky products.
- Best for gifting products.

## Overuse Warnings — Use Sparingly, Not Never

These are permitted words. They become a problem when repeated within a single piece or when they dominate a batch. The fix is always variety and specificity — not removal.

| Word/Phrase | Restriction | Why |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| premium | Max once per piece | Loses meaning through repetition |
| high-quality | Max once per piece. Often unnecessary | Brands and specs speak for themselves |
| best-in-class | Max once per piece | Feels corporate if overused |
| cutting-edge | Max once per piece | Borderline cliché |
| trusted, reliable | Fine on category PLPs (Type A/D). **Do not use on brand PLPs (Type B/C)** | Makes brand-specific copy feel generic and interchangeable |
| enjoy | Fine on category PLPs (Type A/D). **Do not use on brand PLPs (Type B/C)** | Makes brand-specific copy feel generic and interchangeable |
| features | Fine in general copy. **Do not use on brand PLPs (Type B/C)** | Too vague — name the actual feature instead (e.g. TwinDos, BubbleWash, InstaView) |
| upgrade | Only for aspirational products (cooking appliances, phones, TVs). Not for small products (scales, kettles, accessories) | Feels oversold for low-consideration items |
| hassle-free / fuss-free / worry-free | Pick one max per piece. Never stack | Pattern fatigue |
| "across the range" | Use "across a range" instead | "The range" implies all products are included, which is a promo/legal risk |

**Why "trusted," "reliable," "enjoy," and "features" are restricted on brand PLPs:** A user navigating to /samsung/fridges already chose Samsung. Writing "enjoy Samsung's trusted range of features" adds no specific information. These words carry zero entity value and make every brand page feel identical. Replace with actual tech names, product formats, series names, or outcomes specific to that brand.

## Phrasing Principles

### Avoid Repetitive Patterns
The goal of variation is copy that doesn't read like it came from a template. This applies at the piece level and across an entire batch.

- Vary S1 opener angles across a batch: outcome-first, feature-first, format-first, spec-first. Don't open every piece the same way.
- Vary where TGG appears in S2: it can anchor early ("Find [Brand] at The Good Guys for..."), mid, or occasionally late. Don't let one position dominate.
- Vary S2 openers: Find, Shop, Discover, Explore, Choose are all valid. Rotate them. If three consecutive pieces open S2 the same way, rewrite two.
- Vary benefit angles: don't lead every piece with the same angle (e.g. always "big brands" or "range of options").

Pattern repetition is a **batch-level** problem, not a single-piece problem. One piece starting "Stay comfortable..." is fine. Five in a row is a pattern. Scan the full batch before finalising.

### Be Specific — Vague Language Has No SEO or AI Value
AI systems and search engines extract meaning from named entities: brand technologies, product formats, specific capacities, series names. Generic copy is invisible to both.

- Instead of "great features" → name the actual feature (TwinDos, BubbleWash, InstaView, Door-in-Door)
- Instead of "quality brands" → name the brands
- Instead of "a wide range of options" → name the formats or sub-types
- Instead of "advanced technology" → name the technology

Write with definite language. State what a product does, not what it "offers" or "provides."

### Avoid Awkward Phrasing
- Read it aloud mentally. If it sounds like a keyword list disguised as a sentence, rewrite it.
- Don't force keywords into unnatural positions.

### "The Good Guys" Placement
- Include once per piece (unless the format requires otherwise).
- Vary placement naturally — don't always bolt it onto the end.

### "across the range" vs "across a range"
- "across the range" = implies ALL products included (promo/legal risk).
- "across a range" = implies a selection (safe).
- Always use "a range" unless the offer genuinely covers the entire range.
