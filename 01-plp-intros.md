# Process 01: PLP Intro Copy (2-Sentence)

Write two-sentence intro paragraphs for Product Listing Pages on The Good Guys website. Copy must be viewport-safe (≤5 lines on ~360px mobile), SEO-correct, intent-aligned, and human-sounding.

---

## Page Types

Classify every URL before writing:

| Type | URL Pattern | Examples |
|------|-------------|---------|
| A — Generic Category | No brand slug | /audio, /coffee-machines |
| B — Brand Hub | Brand slug only | /samsung, /lg |
| C — Brand + Category | Brand slug + category | /samsung/fridges, /dyson/vacuums |
| D — Promo/Deals | /deals/ path | /deals/black-friday-fridges |

---

## Character Count

- **Range:** 220–250 characters (every letter, space, punctuation mark).
- **Lower end preferred.** Aim for 225–235 where the copy reads naturally at that length.
- **Floor: 220.** Never below — too short to carry enough entity depth.
- **Ceiling: 250.** Hard stop. If S1 runs long, shorten S2 first.
- Don't pad to hit 220. Don't force truncation to stay under 250. If it reads well at 228 or 244, that's correct.

---

## Why this copy format exists — SEO and AI citability

PLP intro copy serves two purposes that traditional marketing copy does not.

**1. Semantic anchor for search engines.** The intro is the only editable free-text block above the product grid. It tells crawlers what the page is definitively about — not just the URL slug, but the intersection of brand + category + specific product types and technologies. A page for /samsung/fridges that only says "Samsung fridges for every home" ranks for far fewer long-tail queries than one that names French door, top mount, Twin Cooling Plus, and Family Hub.

**2. Entity source for AI systems.** AI platforms (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) cite content that uses definite language and high entity density. Each named technology or product sub-type in the copy maps to a potential query the AI might be answering. Copy that names "Door-in-Door storage, InstaView glass panels and Linear Cooling" is citable for three separate query clusters. Copy that says "great cooling technology" is citable for none.

The two-sentence format is deliberately tight — 220–250 characters forces every word to carry either an entity or a structural function. There is no room for filler.

## Writing Rules

### What good PLP intro copy does
- S1 opens with a specific benefit, outcome, or differentiator tied directly to the brand or category — not a generic claim.
- S1 names at least one brand-specific technology, product format, or defining characteristic.
- S2 names 2–3 specific sub-types, series, tech references, or capacity ranges as a tight natural list.
- "The Good Guys" appears once, anchoring the retailer entity to the brand + category.
- Reads like the first line of a useful buying guide — not an ad, not a product description.

### What it avoids
- Vague benefit language: "superior cooling," "advanced technology," "perfect for your needs." These carry no entity value.
- Repetitive patterns across a batch — varied openers, varied TGG placement, varied benefit angles.
- Keyword stuffing — entities are embedded naturally in sentence structure, not listed as comma dumps.
- Any words or phrases on the hard ban list (see `00-tov.md`).
- Brand-banned words on Type B/C pages (see below).

---

## Page Type Specifics

### Type A — Generic Category
- S1: anchor the category and a key benefit or differentiator.
- S2: add breadth — 2–3 brand names and formats or use cases.
- Mention 2–3 brand names. This is the only page type where naming multiple brands in S2 is appropriate.

### Type B — Brand Hub
- Must feel specific to THIS brand — not interchangeable with any other brand hub.
- S1 must name the brand and at least one proprietary technology, product line, or defining characteristic unique to that brand.
- S2 names 2–3 specific product formats, series, or capacity ranges available at TGG.
- No competitor brands. No comparison language.
- No discovery-stage language ("options to suit everyone," "find the right fit," "explore the range") — the user already chose this brand.
- **Do not use:** trusted, reliable, enjoy, features (see `00-tov.md` overuse warnings).

### Type C — Brand + Category
- Combine brand identity with category-specific differentiation.
- S1: brand name + 1–2 specific technologies or formats that distinguish this brand in this category.
- S2: 2–3 specific sub-types, series names, capacities, or product variants available at TGG.
- Brand name in S1. No competitors.
- **Do not use:** trusted, reliable, enjoy, features (see `00-tov.md` overuse warnings).

### Type D — Promo / Deals
- S1 must contain ONE of: `{Category} deals {year}` / `{Category} Black Friday sale {year}` / `{Category} offers {year}`.
- Include year immediately after the intent phrase.
- Do not stack multiple intent phrases.
- Use product entities and features to carry commercial intent — not price language.
- **Year rule:** Currently 2026. Update annually.

---

## Sentence Structure

- **Exactly 2 sentences.** No run-ons disguised as commas. No extra clauses for padding.
- **S2 must be shorter than S1.** Long S2 creates orphaned text blocks on mobile. If S1 runs long, trim S2 first.
- **S1 openers:** Avoid Discover, Explore, Shop as S1 openers — these suit S2. Vary S1 openers across a batch: outcome-first, feature-first, format-first, spec-first angles all work. No single opener should dominate.
- **S2 openers:** Find, Shop, Discover, Explore, Choose are all valid. Rotate across a batch. Don't open three consecutive S2s the same way.

---

## Batch Variation Rules

Scan the full batch before finalising. Flag and rewrite if:
- 3+ pieces open S1 with the same verb or structure
- 3+ pieces open S2 with the same word (Discover, Find, etc.)
- The same benefit angle leads more than half the batch (e.g. always "big brands," always "range of options")
- TGG appears in the same S2 position (early/mid/late) in more than 60% of pieces

---

## Formatting

- Sentence case only (capitalise first word + proper nouns).
- Plain text only — no links, HTML, markdown.
- No exclamation marks. No hard-sell language.
- No encoding artifacts.

---

## Rewrite Guidance

- **Too long (>250):** Shorten S2 first — remove one entity from the list or compress a phrase. Then trim S1 adjectives.
- **Too short (<220):** Add one specific sub-type, tech name, or capacity reference. Don't pad with adjectives.
- **S2 longer than S1:** Move detail to S1 or cut one entity from S2.
- **Sounds like every other piece in the batch:** Change the S1 opener angle entirely — switch from outcome-first to format-first, or feature-first to spec-first.
- **Vague S1:** If S1 could apply to any brand in that category, it's not specific enough. Add the brand's proprietary tech name or a defining product format.
