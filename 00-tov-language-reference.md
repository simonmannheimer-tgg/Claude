# TGG Language & Tone of Voice Reference

> **Version:** 1.0  
> **Last updated:** 2026-02-27  
> **Applies to:** All content-writing processes (01, 02, 03, 05, 08, 09)  
> **Source:** SEM Copy Guide + SEO content standards

## Philosophy

Write copy that sounds like a knowledgeable person wrote it — not an AI, not a template, not a keyword-stuffing machine. The goal is always the best mix of:

1. **User value** — helpful, clear, benefit-driven
2. **AI citability** — entity-rich, structured, specific enough to be quoted
3. **SEO performance** — intent-aligned, keyword-natural
4. **Legal compliance** — no false claims, no misleading framing
5. **Brand alignment** — The Good Guys voice: friendly, direct, Australian

If any two of these conflict, flag it. Don't silently pick one.

## Hard Bans (Never Use)

These words/phrases cannot appear in any TGG content:

| Banned | Why | Use Instead |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| sale / sales | SEM compliance | deal / deals |
| save / saving (as price framing) | SEM compliance | pay less / get a great deal on |
| discount | SEM compliance | deal / offer |
| exclusive (re: deals) | Legal — can't claim deals are exclusive | best deals, great deals |
| Australia's trusted choice | SEM compliance | pay less at The Good Guys |
| quality brands | SEM compliance | big brands, best brands |
| get great value | SEM compliance | get a great deal on [product] |
| perfect for all needs | Too vague | be specific: perfect for your home / perfect for the whole family |
| chat to our staff/team | SEM compliance | chat to our experts in store / chat to our friendly team members |
| amazing, stunning, wonderful | Oversold, generic | be specific about the actual benefit |
| busy homes, hearty meals, sleek design | Cliché, AI-sounding | describe the actual scenario or feature |

### Energy Claims
- "Energy efficient" is OK **only** when the product genuinely is (e.g. heat pump dryers). Do not apply to non-energy-efficient categories.
- Never combine energy claims with "saving money" — this is false green messaging.
- "Efficient dryers" = heat pump dryers only. Not all dryers qualify.

### Price Beat
- Do not use "save with Price Beat" → use "pay less with Price Beat Online" or "get a great deal with Price Beat Online"
- Confirm with TGG team whether Price Beat is applicable to the category before mentioning

### Click & Collect
- Cannot be used on big and bulky products
- Best used around gifting products
- Double check in-store vs online availability for specific products

## Overuse Warnings (Use Sparingly — Not Banned)

These are fine occasionally but become problems when repeated across a batch or within the same page:

| Word/Phrase | Guidance |
|-------------|----------|
| premium | Max once per piece. Don't stack with other superlatives. |
| high-quality | Max once. Often unnecessary — the brands/specs speak for themselves. |
| best-in-class | Max once. Feels corporate if overused. |
| cutting-edge | Max once. Borderline cliché. |
| trusted, reliable | Fine on category PLPs. Banned on brand PLPs (too generic for a brand page). |
| enjoy | Fine on category PLPs. Banned on brand PLPs. |
| features | Fine in general copy. Banned on brand PLPs (too vague — name the actual features). |
| upgrade | Only for aspirational products (cooking appliances, phones, TVs). Not for small products like bathroom scales or kettles. |
| hassle-free / fuss-free / worry-free | Pick one max per piece. Never stack. |
| "across the range" | Implies promo covers the whole range. Use "across a range" instead. |

## Phrasing Principles

### Avoid Repetitive Patterns
- Don't always open with the same structure. If every intro reads "[Verb] [benefit] with [category] at The Good Guys" — it's templated and obviously AI.
- Vary: sentence openers, where TGG appears in the sentence, how benefits are introduced, which angle leads (benefit-first vs product-first vs use-case-first).
- When writing batches, check for pattern repetition across the set — not just within each piece.

### Avoid Awkward Phrasing
- Read it out loud. If it sounds like a chatbot or a keyword list disguised as a sentence, rewrite.
- Don't force keywords into unnatural positions.
- "We Have A Selection Of Styles, Designs And Modern Features On Audio" → bad. "We Have A Great Range Of Audio From All The Big Brands At The Good Guys" → better.

### Be Specific
- Instead of "great features" → name 2–3 actual features
- Instead of "quality brands" → name the brands
- Instead of "all your needs" → name the actual use cases
- Specificity is what makes copy both useful to humans and citable by AI

### "The Good Guys" Placement
- Include once per piece (unless the format requires otherwise)
- Vary placement naturally — don't always put it at the end of S2
- It should read as a natural part of the sentence, not a bolted-on tag

### The "across the range" / "across a range" Rule
- "across the range" = implies ALL products are included (promo/legal risk)
- "across a range" = implies a selection (safe)
- Always use "a range" unless you've confirmed the offer genuinely covers the entire range

## Australian English

All content must use Australian English:
- optimise (not optimize)
- colour (not color)
- organisation (not organization)
- centre (not center)
- favourite (not favorite)
- behaviour (not behavior)
