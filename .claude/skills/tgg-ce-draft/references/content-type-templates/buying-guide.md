# Buying Guide — TGG Augmentation Notes

These augment the base buying-guide template from `tgg-template-generator`. Do not duplicate base template sections here.

---

## TGG-specific sections to include

### Australian retail considerations
Place this section in the lower third of the article (not the intro). Cover:
- Relevant energy star rating for this product category and what rating to target
- Whether finance options are commonly used for this purchase (high-value items: yes)
- Click-and-collect availability for bulky items
- Any Australian Standards relevant to safety or installation

### EAV attributes block
Place this early — by the second or third H2. Format as a table or definition list:

| Attribute | What it means | What to look for |
|---|---|---|
| [Attribute name] | [Plain-English definition] | [Target value or range] |

Minimum 5 attributes. Maximum 10. Cover only attributes buyers genuinely use to decide.

### Recommendation callout
Place before the FAQ section. One short paragraph (3–5 sentences) that directly answers:
- Who should buy the entry-level option
- Who should buy the mid-range option
- Who should buy the premium option (if applicable)

Be specific. Name the differentiating attribute, not the price tier.

---

## Structural guidance

**Intro paragraph:** No heading. State the product category, who it is for, and the one thing buyers most often get wrong when choosing. 2–4 sentences.

**FAQ section:** Place last. 5–8 questions. Draw from the `seo-data.md` related queries. Questions must be natural-language search queries, not formal headings.

**Internal links:** Place 1–2 per H2. Use `[LINK: /slug]` format. Link to subcategory pages, brand pages, and related buying guides — not to product detail pages (those change).
