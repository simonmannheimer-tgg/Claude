# Voice References

Pointers to existing TGG content that the drafting skill can model for tone and structure.
Do not copy text from these pages. Use them to calibrate directness, sentence length, and how TGG talks about products.

These are reference pointers — not authoritative style rules. Voice rules live in `tgg-humanizer` and `simon-voice`.

---

## How to use this file

When Stage 6 hands off to `tgg-seo (strategy mode)`, include this note:

> Before drafting, review the voice reference pages below to calibrate tone. TGG content is direct and practical. It tells customers what to look for and why — not what the brand thinks about its own products. Sentences are short to medium length. No fluff.

## TGG tone markers to replicate

- Opens with the task or the problem — not with a company statement
- Uses active voice ("Choose a model with…" not "A model should be chosen…")
- References real product specs (star ratings, litres, kg, watts) — not vague "powerful" claims
- Mentions Australian context naturally (energy costs, council kerbside, AU standards) without making it the whole point
- Does not end with a promotional call-to-action paragraph — editorial content ends when the information ends

---

## TGG tone markers to avoid

- "We pride ourselves on…" — brand voice, not editorial voice
- "Our team of experts…" — editorial content does not reference TGG staff
- "Whether you're a family or a single professional…" — avoid demographic lists as openers
- "In today's fast-paced world…" — scene-setting cliché
- Any sentence that could appear on a competitors' page unchanged — TGG content must be specific
